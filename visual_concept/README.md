It needs to be simple. I am using JavaFX and GIMP with a mouse.

What about Peppa Pig? I bet I could do a little Peppa Pig with GIMP.

[Overall visual concept](overall_visual_concept.png).

I want maximum readability. I found out, that cards with a little white seam around the edges are less readable than cards without the seam: 
[Cards with seam](cards_with_seam.png)
[Cards without seam](cards_without_seam.png)

I tested three different types of motives:
- Initially the idea was depicting fruits. But the fruits didn't cover the whole card. I figured a motive, or rather a pattern, that covers the whole card is more readable.
- Then I used black and white patterns, as seen in the [overall visual concept](overall_visual_concept.png). The idea was nice, as this would have given color-blind people the same chances. But it was too difficult to play the game. It turns out, that we remember objects that we can grab more easily.
- Lastly, and this is the current version, I used objects from daily life. I made sure that they feature somehow a pattern that I can depict over all of the card, rather than a little shape in the middle of the card. If you will: It's micro-shots rather than macro-shots.
